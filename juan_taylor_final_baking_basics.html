Baking Basics

  • Oven Thermometer to ensure the appliance has hit the exact degree required for a recipe.
  • Hand Mixer or Stand Mixer
  • Cake Pans
  • Glass Square Pan
  • Muffin Pan
  • Loaf Pan
  • Pie Pan
  • Rimmed Baking Sheets are necessary for making cookies, toffee, brittles, rolls, and more
  • Mixing Bowls
  • Measuring Cups and Spoons. Invest in a set of dry and liquid measuring cups and measuring spoons
  • Rolling Pin
  • Cooling rack allows baked goods to cool from all directions, preventing prolonged overcooking and soggy bottoms as a result of trapped moisture.

  • Cookie Cutters
  • Food Processor
  • Pastry Brush
  • Pastry Bags and Piping Tips

Fundamentals

  • When baking accuracy is important
  • Be precise when measuring ingredients
  • Stick to one measuring system when baking
  • Bring cold ingredients to room temperature when making batters and doughs
  • When all the ingredients are at the same temperature, they emulsify easily
  • Ingredients must be mixed in a specific order to achieve the desired results
  • While baking leave room between pans in the oven for air to circulate freely
  • The baking process does not stop once a product is removed from the oven
  • There is residual heat contained in the hot pan and will continue to bake as the product cools
  • It's crucial to pay attention during the last few minutes of baking and remove the baked good at the right moment
  • Baking is a lot of trial and error
  • It requires learning your tools and knowing there limitations
  • Most of all have FUN!
